BENEFITS OF AN ENGINE OVERHAUL THAT JUST MAKE “CENTS”
Overhaul. It sounds daunting, right? But what if the alternative was costly, unanticipated repairs, downtime, and delays? You don’t have to push your engine to the point of failure before considering an overhaul. In fact, planning an overhaul before it’s a do-or-die moment can save you time and money and ensures you’re not stressed and stranded.
WHY OVERHAUL MY ENGINE?
Engines naturally wear down. This is a combination of normal wear and tear, heat, driving style, and time. When an engine reaches midlife, the risk of massive engine failure increases and at this point an overhaul is not only a hassle, but can result in delayed deliveries and added, unanticipated business expenses.

Why wait for failure? A pre-emptive overhaul ensures your engine continues to run when you need it most. Overhauling an engine involves disassembling it, cleaning and inspecting parts, repairing portions of the engine as necessary and reassembling it. Throughout the process, parts are inspected and even minor issues that would otherwise go unnoticed can be addressed, avoiding the potential for bigger issues down the road. Overhauling not only benefits you as a current owner or operator, keeping things running as they should, but it’s the gift that keeps on giving. Completing an engine overhaul adds resale value and appeal to future owners, ensuring them the engine is in top condition with a lot of miles, or kilometers, left in it.
WHEN DO I NEED AN ENGINE OVERHAUL?
There are several clues it may be time to prepare for an engine overhaul. Mileage is the most obvious red flag that it’s time to begin the process. An engine nearing 500,000 miles (804,000 kms) is at an increased risk of catastrophic engine failure and significant repair. But your odometer isn’t the only clue. Watch for oil that smells like fuel. This can be a sign of fuel dilution—when excess unburned fuel mixes with engine oil—altering the viscosity of the motor oil and essentially watering it down. You may also notice abnormal engine noises like pinging or a feeling as though the engine is running roughly. All of these are warning signs your engine may be in need of some major work. But the good news is that an overhaul can address all of these issues and perform other important maintenance at the same time. An engine overhaul may even help improve your fuel consumption and save you money on future repairs and maintenance.
